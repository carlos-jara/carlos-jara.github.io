About

I am an academic researcher in the Department of Natural Sciences and Technology at the University of Aysén (Chile). My research focuses on pressure transients in water distribution networks and their role in pipe deterioration. I also work on machine learning, data analysis, and computational modelling tools for the water sector to support infrastructure understanding and reliability-based decision making.

Prior to joining the University of Aysén, I obtained my PhD from Imperial College London, based in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, under the supervision of Prof. Ivan Stoianov. I also hold a MSc in Environmental Engineering from Imperial College London, and a Bachelor of Engineering and Professional Title in Civil Engineering from Universidad Austral de Chile, including one year abroad at the Technical University of Dresden (Germany).

Before entering academia, I worked in both industry and the public sector, first as a structural and quality assurance engineer and later as a project engineer at the Regional Government of Aysén (Chile).

Research

  • Water distribution systems analysis.
  • Impact of pressure transients on water distribution pipe breaks.
  • Computational modelling tools for the analysis of water distribution networks.
